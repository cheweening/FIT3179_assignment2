World Happiness

The choropleth map above shows the happiness score for each country for year 2019. There are large differences across countries. According to the map above, countries with higher score are Finland, Denmark, Iceland and so on which they have a score above 7. In the same year, the lowest national score corresponding to Afghanistan, Tanzania, Yemen and so on which are having score lesser than 3.5.


Happiness by Continent

Each bar in the bar graph indicates a continent and the height show the average scores of the country in that continent. From the graph we can see that Oceania have the highest average score which is 7.267 and Africa have the lowest score which is 4.368. For this graph we can conclude that people living in Oceania is the happiest compare to the other continent.

Oceania have the highest average score as New Zealand (ranking 8 in 2019), Australia (ranking 10 in 2019) belongs to that continent which contribute to a higher average score.


Top 10 Happiest Countries from 2015 to 2019

Select the countries at the legend to see it's rank over the years.

In the bump chart each colour indicate a country and the countries is ranked according to it's corresponding score over the year. From the bump chart we can observe that Finland have a big incrementation in rank from 2015 to 2019, where it went from the sixth place to the first.

In the bump chart we can also observe that Switzerland have a very obvious drop since year 2015, where it went from the first to the sixth place.
