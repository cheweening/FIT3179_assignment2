World Happiness Report

The World Happiness Report, published by the United Nations' Sustainable Development Solutions Network (SDSN), uses survey data to determine how people in more than 150 countries rate their lives.

How happy are people today? Were people happier before? And how do our living conditions play a role in all of this? These are frequently asked questions about social health.

The Cantril Ladder Score is used to calculate the life ladder score (on a scale of 10). A higher life ladder score denotes a better life, whereas a lower score denotes a worse life.

Relationship Between Happiness and GDP per capita (2019)

In scatter plot, each dot in the visualization represents one country. The vertical position of the visualization shows the GDP per capita based on purchasing power parity, where as the horizontal position shows the score which range from 0 to 10 where 10 is the highest possible score.

This correlation help us to know the relationship between GDP and the happiness score. From the scatter plot we can conclude that countires with higher GDP will have higher happiness score.
